Course Description

This course is a required workshop series designed to prepare students for the Summer Capstone Project. The first workshop focuses on strategies and tactics for solving problems independently using AI. Subsequent workshops cover key topics such as identifying and framing business problems to deliver measurable value, collecting data from APIs and websites, and considering the ethical implications of working with data.

Learning Approach

You will learn the workshop material "the hard way," by making mistakes and finding solutions through trial and error. It is important you follow along with the workshop exercises to learn by doing. By the end of the semester, you will have work that you can reference when needed.

Course Outcomes

By the end of this workshop series, students will be able to:

  1. Independent Problem Solving: Become an independent problem solver using various online resources and collaborative approaches
  2. Data Collection: Collect data from various sources such as APIs and websites using appropriate tools and techniques
  3. Problem Framing: Identify and frame a business problem in a way that can be addressed through data analytics
  4. Ethical Awareness: Recognize the ethical ramifications and privacy concerns that arise throughout the various stages in the data analytics pipeline
  5. Privacy & Safety: Prescribe techniques to ensure privacy and to better safeguard against unintended consequences associated with data-related activities

How Are You Evaluated?

This workshop series uses a Credit/No Credit grading system rather than traditional letter grades.

Workshop Deliverables

For each workshop, you will complete and submit a set of assigned exercises. The deliverables are evaluated based on:

  • Completion: Did you complete all required exercises?
  • Effort: Did you demonstrate genuine engagement with the material?
  • Learning: Does your work show progress and understanding?

Submission Policy

On-Time Definition: If workshop deliverables are submitted less than 2 days late, they are considered on time.

Grace Period: This policy recognizes that workshops are intensive and allows for reasonable flexibility in submission timing.

Academic Integrity Policy

Academic dishonesty will be treated as an extremely serious matter with severe consequences that can range from receiving no credit for assignments/tests, failing the class, to expulsion. It is never permissible to turn in any work that has not been authored by the student, such as work that has been copied from another student or copied from a source (including the Internet) without properly acknowledging the source.

It is your responsibility to make sure that your work meets the standard set forth in the LMU Academic Honesty Policy.
